A

How to use this brief

Read this page once, top to bottom, before you open the timeline
What you're making

10 short, sound-off, UGC-style direct-response ads for Meta. Each is engineered to stop the scroll in 3 seconds, prove one idea, and drive to the offer. They are not brand films — speed and clarity beat polish.

Priority order

Build Scripts 01, 02, 03, 07 first — these are the four prospecting workhorses. Then 04/05/08 (UGC variants), then 06/09/10 (condition & convenience spins). Each script header tells you its role.

How to read a script

Each has a shot table (Time · Visual · Voiceover · On-screen text) plus a production block (B-roll, SFX/music, hook variants, editor notes). VO and on-screen text are verbatim — type them exactly. The ▶ Reference ad link plays the real competitor ad it's modeled on.

The single most important rule

Every ad lives or dies on the first 3 seconds. Spoken hook + bold on-screen text + a visual pattern-interrupt must all land before 0:03. If a viewer doesn't stop, nothing after it matters. When in doubt, cut the intro and start on the hook.

B

Global production specs

Applies to all 10 unless a script says otherwise

Format & export

  • Master aspect: 9:16 vertical — 1080 × 1920
  • Frame rate: 30 fps (match source UGC; don't mix)
  • Codec: H.264 .mp4, ~10–12 Mbps, AAC audio
  • Loudness: normalize VO to −14 to −16 LUFS; no clipping
  • Length: prospecting 20–35s; VSL (Script 07) 60–90s

Safe zones (Reels/Stories UI)

  • Keep all text & logo inside the center 1080 × 1350 band
  • Top ~250px and bottom ~420px are covered by platform UI — keep them clear of captions/CTA
  • Design so a 1:1 and 4:5 crop still works (no edge-critical text)

Captions (always on)

  • Burn in subtitles on 100% of dialogue — most viewers watch muted
  • Font Figtree / Silka Bold, white with a soft dark stroke or pill background for legibility
  • Max 2 lines, large, centered low-third (above the UI safe zone)
  • Highlight the key claim word in [vrsn] lime #b8de55 as it's spoken

Pacing, motion & SFX

  • Cut every 1.5–3s; punch-in zoom on each key claim
  • Hook text animates in hard (no slow fades) in first 0:03
  • SFX: subtle whoosh on cuts, "ding" on ✅ checkmarks, a satisfying "squish/foam" on the cushion demo
  • Music: licensed, warm & upbeat, low energy bed; duck −12dB under VO. No copyrighted tracks.

CTA end-card (last 2–3s of every ad)

[vrsn] wordmark on white/cream → product shot → button graphic Shop Now. On-screen offer lockup: 40% OFF + Free Washbag ($39) · 100-Day Risk-Free. VO closes on the guarantee. Keep it under 3 seconds — the CTA is a stamp, not a scene.

Whenever a script calls for the cushion demo, shoot/edit the same recurring motif: (1) foot sinking into something too soft (marshmallow/foam) → (2) foot on something too hard (concrete/jarring) → (3) the [vrsn] balanced middle. This visual is the brand's signature — reuse it as a consistent thumbnail across variants.

D

Claims & legal guardrails

Meta gets strict on health/footwear claims — stay inside these lines

✅ Safe to say

  • “Helps relieve / eases foot, heel & everyday pain,” “all-day comfort,” “supports natural movement”
  • “Podiatrist-recommended,” “wide toe box,” “easy slip-on” — these are existing [vrsn] claims
  • “100-day risk-free,” “40% off,” “free washbag” — confirm the offer is live before burning it in
  • Real customer testimonials with an on-screen “#ad” / “paid partnership” disclosure

⛔ Avoid / get sign-off

  • No cure/treatment claims — don't say [vrsn] "cures," "heals," or "treats" plantar fasciitis, neuropathy, diabetes, arthritis
  • No "instant" / "guaranteed" medical results; prefer "most feel relief from the first walk"
  • No before/after that implies a medical outcome; no doctor in a white coat unless they're a real, credited podiatrist
  • Add “Results vary” super on any testimonial that names a result
  • Don't invent stats or review counts — use only numbers the brand confirms

Rule of thumb

Describe how the shoe is built and how people feel — never diagnose or promise to fix a medical condition. When a line feels borderline, flag it for brand/legal sign-off before you finish the cut.

  • Personas: nurse (scrubs), 50–65 woman, active man
  • "Foot pain" reaction b-roll (rubbing heel/knee/back)
  • Real customer selfie-testimonial clips + #ad disclosure

Graphics & audio

  • [vrsn] logo (SVG/PNG, light + dark)
  • Offer end-card template (40% off · free washbag · 100-day)
  • Review screenshots / star-rating supers (brand-approved)
  • Demo props: foam/marshmallow + a hard surface (Goldilocks)
  • Licensed music + SFX pack

The "Hook variants to test" listed on each shot sheet are the starting set. Build these fast and cheap; that's how this category scales winners.
